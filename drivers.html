Carlos Sainz Sr.

Carlos Sainz Cenamor is a living rally legend.
And even though he only won two WRC championship titles — which is still an objectively amazing achievement —Sainz finished a handful of seasons in third or second place, sometimes just shy of the title.

Fernando Alonso

Fernando Alonso is undoubtedly one of the most talented drivers walking the earth today.
Alonso proved his abilities, starting in the seat of a go-kart and climbing his way up the race latter, until ultimately making his F1 debut in 2001 and then winning back-to-back F1 titles in 2005 and 2006.

Richard Petty

Richard is the son of Lee Petty, a three-time NASCAR champion and winner of the inaugural Daytona 500 in 1959. Petty is tied with Dale Earnhardt and Jimmie Johnson for the most NASCAR Cup Championship wins with
a total of seven — though Petty was the first to complete the feat. Seldom seen without a cowboy hat or auto racing helmet.

Tom Kristensen

Tom climbed his way through the ranks, taking victories in the Japanese and German Formula 3 series,
as well as in Formula 3000, the British Touring Car Championship. Kristensen also holds records for most wins at other elite endurance events including the 12 Hours of Sebring where he has half-a-dozen wins.
